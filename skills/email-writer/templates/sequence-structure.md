# Sequence Structure

Use this template when building multi-email outreach sequences. For single emails, this file is not needed.

---

## 3-Email Sequence (Standard)

The default. Works for most cold outreach campaigns.

### Email 1: Introduce + Poke
- **Subject:** 2-3 words, lowercase, colleague-style
- **Line 1:** "Why you, why now" — shows research, appears in preview
- **Lines 2-3:** Poke the bear question OR state the problem/opportunity
- **Line 4:** Social proof (quantified, specific)
- **Line 5:** CTA (answer: "How do we make this worth their time?")
- **Word count:** Under 100

### Email 2: Expand + Different Angle (Threaded to Email 1)
- No new subject line — reply in thread
- **Line 1:** Personalized opener (AI-generated line, analogy, recent news, something new)
- **Lines 2-3:** Deeper explanation of how you help OR different value prop angle
- **Line 4:** Different case study or proof point
- **Line 5:** Similar CTA to Email 1
- **Word count:** Under 80

### Email 3: Lower Friction + New Value Prop (New Thread)
- **Subject:** New subject line — this should feel like a fresh email, not a follow-up
- **Line 1:** Different "why you, why now" line
- **Lines 2-3:** Switch value prop (if Email 1 was "save time," Email 3 is "save money" or "reduce risk")
- **Line 4:** New social proof
- **Line 5:** Lower friction CTA (video walkthrough, case study, free audit — not a call)
- **Word count:** Under 80

### Value Prop Rotation

| Email | Value Prop Focus | CTA Intensity |
|-------|-----------------|---------------|
| Email 1 | Primary (usually time savings or efficiency) | Standard — call or meeting |
| Email 2 | Same or expanded — deeper proof | Standard — similar to Email 1 |
| Email 3 | Different (money, risk, competitive advantage) | Lower — video, resource, audit |

## 4-Email Sequence (With Breakup)

Add a breakup email for maximum conversion. Breakup emails consistently get the highest response rates because they remove pressure.

### Emails 1-3: Same as above

### Email 4: Breakup (Threaded to Email 3)
- No new subject line — reply in thread
- **Structure:** "I've reached out a few times. Assuming the timing isn't right. No hard feelings."
- **Optional redirect:** "Should I reach out to [other person on their team] instead?"
- **Word count:** Under 50

**The breakup works because:**
- Signals you won't keep following up (removes annoyance)
- Creates scarcity (last chance to engage)
- Shows confidence (you don't need this deal)
- The redirect to a colleague often gets a response ("No, I'm the right person, just been busy")

## 5-Touch Multi-Channel Sequence

For high-value targets where email alone isn't enough.

| Touch | Channel | Purpose |
|-------|---------|---------|
| 1 | Email | Cold outreach (Email 1 structure) |
| 2 | LinkedIn | Connection request + note referencing Email 1 topic |
| 3 | Email | Threaded follow-up (Email 2 structure) |
| 4 | Phone | Call — reference emails and LinkedIn. Leave voicemail if no answer. |
| 5 | Email | New thread (Email 3 structure) or breakup |

**Timing:**
- Touch 1-2: Same day or next day
- Touch 3: Day 3-4
- Touch 4: Day 5-7
- Touch 5: Day 10-14

---

## Sequence Rules

1. **Email 2 always threads to Email 1.** Reminds them they didn't respond, which can work if Email 1 was strong.
2. **Email 3 always starts a new thread.** Feels like a fresh email, not a desperate follow-up.
3. **Email 4 (breakup) threads to Email 3.** Keeps the most recent conversation in one thread.
4. **If Email 3 outperforms Email 1, swap them.** Test this in your first 50-100 sends.
5. **Each email should be shorter than the last.** You've already said the main thing. Don't add, subtract.
6. **Vary the value prop across emails.** Don't say the same thing three ways. Say three different things.
7. **Never reference previous emails in the sequence.** Each email should stand alone. "As I mentioned in my last email..." is a dead giveaway it's a sequence.
